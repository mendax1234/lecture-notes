# Semiconductor Technologies and Circuit Taxonomy

It is obvious that the choice of a **semiconductor technology** (CMOS, Bipolar, and BiCMOS for silicon subtrates) and the **circuit class** affect the type of **CAD tools** that are required.

* The choice of a **semiconductor technology** affects mainly the **physical design** of a circuit, i.e., the determination of the **geometric patterns**. It affects only marginally the **functional design**.
* Conversely, the **circuit type** (i.e., **analog**, **synchronous digital**, or **asynchronous digital**) affects the **overall design** of the circuit.

In this book we consider **CAD techniques** for **synchronous digital circuits**, because they represent the **vast majority of circuits** and because these **CAD design techniques** have reached a **high level of maturity**.
