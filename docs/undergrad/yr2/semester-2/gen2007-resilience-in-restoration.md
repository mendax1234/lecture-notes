## Course Content

As a mainly hands-on course, this module introduces us to different types of ecosystems, including rainforests, swamp forests, mangroves, and rivers. And most importantly, the restoration ecology. However, as someone who is not a geography/BES major, I am not sure if I understand the course purpose correctly or not :joy\_cat:.

For those participating in fieldwork, it is important to attend the pre-trip fieldwork training session. The training helps us learn how to use the necessary tools and better understand what to expect during fieldwork.

## Teaching Style and Materials

### Teaching Sylte

#### Lecture

There are four lectures in total throughout the semester, each held on Thursday evening monthly from 6 pm to 9 pm. Prof. Gretchen is very passionate about this course, and her enthusiasm can clearly be felt in every lecture she delivers!

At the end of each lecture, Prof. Gretchen also treats the class to dinner, usually pizza or sushi depending on our preferences. This makes the evening lectures feel much more enjoyable!

#### Field Trip

This is definitely the highlight of the course. The 17-day field trip to Sabah was an amazing experience, allowing us to observe different ecosystems in person and engage with local communities through hands-on restoration work.

Overall, it was a very meaningful and interesting trip that helped me better appreciate the importance of ecosystem restoration in real-world contexts.

#### Assessments

* **Online quiz:** The online quiz is held before the start of the Sabah trip. For my cohort, it was an MCQ quiz on Canvas and was very manageable (some people experienced the Canvas hack so they did the hand-written version, which is much harder for me I think). However, I believe Prof. Gretchen may change the assessment format in future offerings of the course.
* **Field notebook:** This was my first time keeping a field notebook, which I realized is an essential skill for ecologists. I really enjoyed drawing what I observed in the field. My suggestion is to follow the daily prompts given by Prof. Gretchen, while also noting down your own observations and reflections. Doing this consistently should be more than sufficient.
* **Group project:** There were five group projects in my cohort, all of which were very interesting and covered different aspects of resilient restoration. We could choose the project we wanted to work on before the Sabah trip. During the trip, we also had opportunities to learn about and experience what other groups were doing.
* **IG Reel:** After the Sabah trip, we were also required to create an IG Reel to demonstrate our understanding of resilient restoration and help promote the restoration work in Sabah to a wider audience.
